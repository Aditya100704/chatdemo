How the RenterFinder Referral Programme Works — Earn ₹500 Per Deal, Pan-India

The RenterFinder.com Refer & Earn programme is open to anyone across India — whether you are a landlord, a renter, a housing society member, a resident welfare association, a real estate professional, or simply someone who knows people looking for rental properties or trustworthy tenants. There is no upper limit on the number of referrals you can make, and no cap on the total amount you can earn. Every completed deal that uses your referral code pays you ₹500, regardless of the city, property type, or rent amount involved.

To participate, contact us at renterfinder1@gmail.com with your Name, Date of Birth, Address, Aadhaar Number, Mobile Number, and Email ID. We will issue you a unique referral code that you can share with anyone looking to rent a property or find a genuine tenant. When your referred contacts register on RenterFinder.com using your code, they automatically receive a discounted Platform Service Fee of 10 days' rent instead of the standard 12 days' rent — saving them money and giving them a direct incentive to use your code.

RenterFinder.com operates as India's only rental-exclusive platform, connecting landlords and renters directly without any broker involvement. Both landlords and renters pay a Profile Listing Fee of ₹125 for 3 months, and a Platform Service Fee of 12 days' rent split into 6 days to arrange the property meeting and 6 days at deal closure. Because the platform covers every city and town across India — from metros like Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, and Pune to tier-2 cities like Jaipur, Lucknow, Patna, Bhopal, Ranchi, Nagpur, and beyond — your referral network can span the entire country without any geographic restriction.

Your ₹500 reward is paid after the deal is finalised and the full Platform Service Fee has been received by RenterFinder.com from the referred client. There is no joining fee, no subscription, and no upfront cost to join the programme. The referral programme is particularly well suited to anyone already embedded in the rental ecosystem — housing society secretaries, relocation professionals, HR managers who help new joiners find accommodation, student placement coordinators, and individuals who regularly encounter people relocating for work or study.
